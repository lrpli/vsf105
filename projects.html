What We Do

From free first flights for kids to community fly-ins, VSF105 keeps aviation alive and thriving in East Tennessee.

Featured Program

Young Eagles — Our Flagship

✈ EAA Young Eagles Program

First Flights That Change Lives

Young Eagles is the world's largest youth aviation program — and VSF105 is proud to be an active chapter. We provide free airplane rides to children ages 8–17, introducing them to general aviation and the possibilities that come with it.

No experience needed. Just curiosity, enthusiasm, and a desire to look at the world from 3,000 feet. Volunteer pilots donate their time and aircraft to make every flight possible.

Ways to Get Involved

Whether you fly, wrench, or just love watching airplanes, there's a program at VSF105 for you.

Training

Pilot Training Support

VSF105 connects student pilots with experienced Certificated Flight Instructors (CFIs), provides ground school study groups, and offers mentorship from active members who have navigated every certificate from Sport Pilot to ATP.

Open to: All skill levels
Cost: Member benefit
Events

Annual Fly-In & Open House

Every summer, VSF105 hosts a signature fly-in and open house that draws pilots and aviation fans from across East Tennessee and the greater Southeast. Enjoy aircraft displays, food, and a day of everything aviation.

Frequency: Annual (Summer)
Open to: Public
Build

Homebuilder's Workshop

For the EAA builders among us — the chapter supports experimental aircraft construction and restoration projects through shared workspace, tool access, and the collective expertise of experienced builders.

Open to: Members
Meetings: Monthly
Service

Community Outreach Flights

VSF105 pilots volunteer for community service missions including medical supply transport, search and rescue support coordination, and participation in state emergency response networks.

Open to: Certificated pilots
Type: Volunteer
Social

Chapter Meetups & $100 Hamburgers

Monthly chapter meetings, hangar talks, and informal $100 hamburger fly-outs to destinations around Tennessee and the Southeast. This is where friendships and aviation stories are made.

Frequency: Monthly+
Open to: All members
Education

Aviation Safety Seminars

Regular safety seminars featuring FAA Wings credit opportunities, weather briefings, airspace refreshers, and guest presentations from air traffic controllers, experienced pilots, and aviation safety experts.

FAA Wings: Credit available
Cost: Free to attend
